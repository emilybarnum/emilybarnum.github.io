JMU Queue


From January to May 2023, I had the opportunity to work on an interdisciplinary team in building the office hours queue management system, "JMU Queue". My official role on this team was lead frontend developer. I worked closely with the backend and database developers as well as the UX researchers to deliver a product that was ranked as high quality by our professors and clients.

This web application allows students to join a queue for a professor's office hours and wait anywhere in the building, not just in the hallway by a professor's door. Professors and students can create accounts on this application. Professors have the functionality of uploading their profile picture so students can recognize them quickly upon searching. They can also add/change their office hours availability and purposes for meetings. They can see who joins their office hours, mark them as absent or arrived, and notify them. Students can search for professors, join their queue, and check notifications.

I am proud to be able to show off the results of several months of hard work. During this project, I became comfortable with facilitating conversations in an interdisciplinary team between developers and UX researchers, gathering requirements from clients, and familiarizing myself with Bootstrap to create an intuitive interface.



Software used - Microsoft Visual Studio and Microsoft SQL Server Management Studio

Backend Development - C#

Frontend Development - HTML, CSS, and Bootstrap
